## About Me

**Noah Eilken**

I am a Data Scientist focused on building machine learning models, ETL pipelines, and analytics workflows across public health, finance, and lab operations. My work centers on turning messy, high-dimensional data into forecasting systems, production-ready pipelines, and decision-support tools.

I am currently completing a Master's in Data Science at Harvard University Extension School, along with graduate certificates in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence, and I hold a BS in Biology from Emory University. My graduate research focuses on computational epidemiology, and my broader work has included infectious disease forecasting, nonprofit financial analytics, laboratory operations, and clinical research.

My academic background in microbiome and ecology data analysis, as well as cancer and clinical research, shaped how I approach high-dimensional biological data, experimental analysis, and applied modeling in health-related domains. Across research and industry roles, I have worked in statistical modeling, machine learning, NLP, and data visualization using Python, SQL, AWS, and GCP to deliver analyses and systems with operational impact.
