You may use data other than the Competition Data (“External Data”) to develop and test your Submissions. The use of external data and models is acceptable unless specifically prohibited by the Host. Because of the potential costs or restrictions, for example geo restrictions, associated with obtaining rights to use external data or certain software and associated tools, their use must be reasonably accessible to all and of minimal cost.

The Host will assess whether a particular LLM, dataset, or tool should be excluded based on whether participants are being excluded because of excessive access costs. The Host will apply a Reasonableness Standard in light of factors like cost thresholds and accessibility.

By way of example only, a small subscription charge to use additional elements of a large language model such as Gemini Advanced may be acceptable if it meets the Reasonableness Standard. Purchasing a license to use a proprietary dataset that exceeds the cost of a prize in the competition would not be considered reasonable.

Individual Participants and Teams may use automated machine learning tool(s) (“AMLT”), such as Google toML or H2O Driverless AI, to create a Submission, provided that the Participant or Team ensures that they have an appropriate license to the AMLT such that they are able to comply with the Competition Rules.
